Rehearsal Application

Design

My biggest contribution to the rehearsal application is my redesign of the administrative conversation thread. This view is arguably the most important view of the administrative product and I was beyond excited to be given such an important task. Rehearsal is a tool facilitating asychnronous conversations between mentors and learners. The admin conversation thread is the where mentors go to view learner responses and leave feedback and grades. The nature of this task makes it very tedious, as each mentor has multiple learners responding to each scenario in a very similar way. One main goal was to make this task as painless as possible. By implementing shortcuts such as easy and intuitive navigation between learner responses, I aimed to reduce the friction involved in watching and responding to multiple responses.

One main improvement to the admin thread is the sticky top bar. Since these threads are long and relatively similar to eachother, it is important that the mentor is aware of who is responding to what scenario. By displaying this data concisely and keeping it in the users view at all times instead of at the top of the page as it is currently, we are ensuring the mentor never looses track of the current conversation and is able to maintain an uninterupted workflow.

One issue with the current thread is that users will type thier feedback but forget to press submit. By using a bright bold color for the submit button and an additional warning message, users should be more aware of thier current publication status. In the previous thread, record and type options were displayed inline. In the redesign, they are stacked with video on top, encouraging video feedback from mentors. The entire product is heavily video based, therefore we want to encourage mentors to respond with video, just as their learners are.

In the original design there was a lot of confusion about Comments, Prompts and Feedback. Comment ... while prompts ... and feedback ... While commenting is and action tied to an individual response and prompting and feedbacking are tied to the thread in general, I decided display them in the same way in the same location to better juxtapose thier purposes and indended uses. Since commenting is the least encouraged response it is placed last, hopfully encouraging prompting and leaving feedback first.

Prototyping

After completing the UX and Visual Designs, I created a fully functional prototype with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. In order to avoid design degredation as much as possible, I take my prototypes as far as possible, showing every possible state as well as every animation and interaction. I then passed the prototype on to the front end developers who convert the prototype to React while preserving as much detail as they can.
